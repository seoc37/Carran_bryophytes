Abstract


Carran turlough is a large turlough (90.3 ha) with two basins in the East Burren Special Area of Conservation. This project studied the Fahee basin to the north-east. Carran is unusual in having a layer of peat over much of its area, despite being located in a karst landscape. The objectives of the project were to determine the bryophyte species in Carran turlough and identify possible bryophyte communities. Some aspects of the project were constrained by high water levels during the survey, and by limited access because of farming activity.

Bryophytes are a large and diverse group of plants of ecological and evolutionary importance, divided into mosses (Bryophyta), liverworts (Marchantiophyta) and hornworts (Anthocerotophyta). Their features distinguish them from vascular plants.

Relevés of 0.5m×0.5m were chosen at 54 points. The bryophyte species present were recorded for each relevé using the Domin scale and samples were collected. The positions of relevés and water sampling points were recorded using GPS and mapped using satellite photographs. Previous surveys of the turlough were used to identify vegetation types.

Seven moss species were found. Two (Sphagnum subnitens and Aulacomnium palustre) occurred in only one relevé each and were excluded from later analysis. The other species found were Calliergonella cuspidata, Ctenidium molluscum, Cinclidotus fontinaloides, Rhizomnium punctatum and Rhytidiadelphus triquetrus. It was found that C. cuspidata was very widespread in the turlough and dominant in most relevés. C. molluscum was also widespread but less abundant. C. fontinaloides was found at the edges of the area subject to inundation. R. punctatum was widespread but abundant only in very wet areas. R. triquetrus was found in drier areas. One liverwort species, Riccia sorocarpa, was recorded at a single location. A number of other species associated with turloughs were not recorded, but this may be due to limitations of the survey rather than absence.

The data on species were analysed using Non-Metric Multidimensional Scaling with PC-ORD software. Cluster analysis and indicator species analysis identifiedfour assemblages. The Calliergonella cuspidata assemblage was found throughout the survey area and was dominated by C. cuspidata with some R. punctatum. The Ctenidium molluscum-Calliergonalla cuspidata assemblage was found in more calcicole areas. The Cinclidotus fontinaloides assemblage was found in drier areas near the edges of the turlough. The Rhizomnium punctatum-Calliergonella cuspidata assemblage occurred in the wetter areas. Consideration of habitat indicators suggested that moisture is the most important factor defining the assemblages observed, with light and perhaps some slight local phosphorous enrichment also being influences. No clear association was found between vascular plant communities and bryophyte communities.

Water samples from 15 points were analysed for pH, alkalinity, total phosphorous and soluble reactive phosphorous. The pH of a flush entering the north side of the basin was also assessed. Analysis found that pH and alkalinity in Carran turlough were similar to those in other oligotrophic turloughs, and that water quality was within national limits. The flush was slightly more acidic than water in the turlough itself, probably because it passes through peat soil.

The previously-reported presence of Drosera rotundifolia was confirmed. The presence of this, and of S. subnitens, is anomalous since both species are usually found in acidic habitats.

The project results give an initial assessment of the bryophyte diversity and communities present, and suggest no major changes to the environmental status of the turlough since 2014. Some possible directions for future research are suggested based on the project results.



Keywords: County Clare, Burren, Carran, turlough, bryophyte, moss, liverwort, plant communities.
