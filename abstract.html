Abstract


Carran turlough is a large (90.3 ha in area) turlough located in the East Burren Special Area of Conservation. It consists of two basins. Carran is unusual in having a layer of peat over much of its area, despite being located in a karst landscape. This project studied the Fahee basin to the north-east.

Bryophytes are a large and diverse group of plants of ecological and evolutionary importance, divided into mosses (Bryophyta), liverworts (Marchantiophyta) and hornworts (Anthocerotophyta). Their features distinguish them from vascular plants.

The objectives of the project were to determine the bryophyte species present in Carran turlough and identify possible communities and environmental influences forming them.

Relevés of 0.5m×0.5m were chosen at 54 points. The bryophyte species present were recorded for each relevé using the Domin scale and samples were collected. The positions of relevés and water sampling points were recorded using GPS and mapped using satellite photographs. Previous surveys of the turlough were used to identify vegetation types.

In total, seven moss species were found. Of these, two (Sphagnum subnitens and Aulacomnium palustre) occurred in only one relevé each and were excluded from later analysis. The other species found were Calliergonella cuspidata, Ctenidium molluscum, Cinclidotus fontinaloides, Rhizomnium punctatum and Rhytidiadelphus triquetrus. It was found that C. cuspidata was very widespread in the turlough and dominant in most relevés. C. molluscum was also widespread but less abundant. C. fontinaloides was found at the edges of the area subject to inundation. R. punctatum was widespread but abundant only in very wet areas. R. triquetrus was found in drier areas. One liverwort species, Riccia sorocarpa, was recorded at a single location. A number of other species associated with turloughs were not recorded, but this may be due to limitations of the survey rather than absence.

The data on species were analysed using Non-Metric Multidimensional Scaling with PC-ORD version 5 software. Cluster analysis and indicator species analysis were used to identify four assemblages. The Calliergonella cuspidata assemblage was found throughout the survey area and was dominated by C. cuspidata with some R. punctatum. The Ctenidium molluscum-Calliergonalla cuspidata assemblage was found in more calcicole areas and also included R. punctatum. The Cinclidotus fontinaloides assemblage was found in drier areas near the edges of the turlough. The Rhizomnium punctatum-Calliergonella cuspidata assemblage occurred in the wetter areas. Consideration of habitat indicators suggested that moisture is the most important factor defining the assemblages observed, with light and perhaps some slight local phosphorous enrichment also being influences.

Water samples were collected at 15 points in the turlough and analysed for pH, alkalinity, total phosphorous and soluble reactive phosphorous. The pH of a flush entering the north side of the basin was also assessed. Analysis of the samples shows that conditions of pH and alkalinity in Carran turlough were similar to those in other turloughs, and that water quality was within national limits for phosphorous. The flush was found to be more acidic than water in the turlough itself, probably because it passes through peat soil.

The previously-reported presence of Drosera rotundifolia was confirmed. The presence of this, and of S. subnitens, is probably due to the history of Carran turlough as a bog since both species are usually found in that habitat.

Some aspects of the project were constrained by high water levels during the survey period, and by limited access to some areas of the turlough because of farming activity.

The results of the project provide an initial assessment of the bryophyte diversity and communities present, as well as a snapshot of some environmental characteristics. They also support the view that no major changes to the environmental status of the turlough have happened since the last survey in 2014.



Keywords: County Clare, Burren, Carran, turlough, bryophyte, moss, liverwort, plant communities.
